A Window on the World

   window on the world - a means of experiencing or learning about parts of the world
   beyond one's immediate surroundings or situation.

    "Kids are spoiled rotten with the amount they can learn and see on the internet.
    In my day, the only window on the world was whatever books the local library had."

See also: on, window, world
    Farlex Dictionary of Idioms. © 2022 Farlex, Inc, all rights reserved.

    a window on the world, a way of learning about other people and other countries:
   News programmes try to provide a window on the world.

                                RAIL AT
            ——————————————

Rail at (one) - to criticize, upbraid, or berate one severely and bitterly, especially at length.

  "Humiliated by his lowly position and poor treatment at work, Tom began railing at his family every evening when he got home.
  The boss started railing at Thomas in front of the whole office for messing up the Robertson accounts."

See also: rail

   Farlex Dictionary of Idioms. © 2022 Farlex, Inc, all rights reserved.

   rail at someone (about something)
  to complain loudly or violently to someone about something. Jane railed at the payroll clerk about not
  having received her check. I am not responsible for your problems. Don't rail at me!

See also: rail

  McGraw-Hill Dictionary of American Idioms and Phrasal Verbs. © 2002 by The McGraw-Hill Companies, Inc.

   rail at (v.) - to criticize someone or something in harsh, bitter, or abusive language:
   The workers railed at the new contract that cut medical benefits.
