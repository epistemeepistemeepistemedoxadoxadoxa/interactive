GREAT HILL NY, NY/Air

SUMMER

sometimes in the summer we'd sleep under layers of those heavy sheets that reminded me of november and of home.

of slow, hopeful days -- my own bed and a moment's escape from a frigid loneliness that made my thumbs numb and reduced feeling from
something that existed at hair's tip to just the most vital and centermost threshold of my body

on warmer days we'd create distance and try to find room to breathe, to let the breeze pass over the both of us

we'd try to keep the cool air in

and i didn't have ac
at twenty-two, i couldn't eat an ac

i didn't know how to keep cool air in, how to control that which ebbed and flowed from near and far through cracks and crevices
and even thick brick walls -- that from which there was no escape like time, like language, like style

and our cooking would make the whole place hot, the shitty fan i had stolen after meeting you wasn't enough for that

after long days of the sun's light shimmering along the cracks and splinters of my room's shattered glass

it'd be unbearably hot -- i assume it was

i had grow use to nights where i'd sweat profusely and dream vividly of being carried off in currents and I'd dream of sex and violence
when i awoke, my sweat, like piss, nicotine spit, or big bullet-sized raindrops --
a purification

tomorrow, it'd be 67° and i hoped thunderstorms would bring us closer
the room would be cool, the fan at its very best. it'd be crisp like fall, leaves falling on cobblestone, fire crackling, bells ringing, books shutting
and that reminded me, soon we wouldn't have to worry about heat. there'd be an immeasurable distance between us and we wouldn't have to worry about things being too hot.

and briefly, what we'd grown use to fearing would be gone and all would be well but only briefly. soon we'd be cold, a frigid loneliness eating away at nerve endings and we'd wish it were warm and we'd wish we could sweat in the same way and just sweat it all out. but maybe you'd go somewhere warm where thick blankets were of no use and maybe you'd figure it all out and things would be
okay, pure.



 

 

 

GREAT HILL NY, NY/Questionnaire

FALL

We spent a few days in the rain dancing. Dreamt of green hills growing out from ground which was cold and hard at the moment.

A few nights drinking, warm embraces. A sweet, smoke smell coming in breaths, sighs, laughs, when you spoke. Something familiar, settled, soothing.

I fear that we’d not be able to speak to each other sober. I fear that if we did the death would come by a thousand questions. That you could not know me. You did not know me. Inevitable, the destruction, even if you did.

You asked a few questions. They never got to the heart of anything. Who were you supposed to be? Who was I?

You definitely weren’t anything like me. And this place, these people, they were not my own. Not for me.

I wanted to say to you; Imagine a barren place, a tundra’s tanned earth, snow falling slowly from grey skies. Mountains in the distance. Cold air passes over your ears and meets your face in infinity. In that moment, alone with your eyes closed, looking through it all, who are you? What do you think you look like? With no direction and nothing human, no warmth, in sight: Where is home? What is hope? What is home? Where to start?

Could home be a ray of light venturing through the mundane, touching your face, saying hello for the first time? Sun slipping through curtains and closed doors to warmly embrace us right where we stand? Where to start?
